  1. History
    1. Background

      The IPL (Indian Premier League) revolutionized cricket by blending sports and entertainment, attracting global audiences.It introduced franchise-based teams, player auctions, and a fast-paced T20 format that transformed the game's commercial and competitive landscape.

    2. Foundation

      Established by BCCI in 2007: The IPL was founded by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) in September 2007, with the aim to promote domestic T20 cricket and capitalize on the growing popularity of the shorter format.

    3. Expansions and termination

      New teams like Pune Warriors, Kochi Tuskers, Gujarat Titans, and Lucknow Super Giants were added in different seasons. Teams like Deccan Chargers, Kochi Tuskers Kerala, and Pune Warriors were terminated due to financial or legal issues.

  3. Organisation

    1. Tournament format

      Teams play in a double round-robin format, facing each other twice (home and away). The top 4 teams enter the playoffs, which include Qualifier 1, Eliminator, Qualifier 2, and the Final.

    2. Player acquisition, squad composition and salaries

      Players are acquired through auctions, drafts, or transfers, with teams having a fixed salary cap. Each team must have a mix of Indian and overseas players, with a maximum of 4 overseas players in the playing XI.

    3. Match rules

      Each team plays 20thovers (T20 format), with a maximum of 4 overs per bowler. A team can have only 4 overseas players in the playing XI. The Powerplay is the first 6th overs, where only 2 fielders are allowed outside the 30-yard circle. DRS (Decision Review System) is allowed for checking dismissals. In case of a tie, a Super Over is played to decide the winner.

    4. Prize money

      Winner (CSK) received ₹20 crore; Runner-up (SRH) got ₹12.5 crore. Orange Cap (Kane Williamson) and Purple Cap (Andrew Tye) winners got ₹10 lakh each. Most Valuable Player (Sunil Narine) and Emerging Player (Rishabh Pant) also received ₹10 lakh each. Super Striker of the Season (Sunil Narine) won a TATA Nexon car instead of cash. Total prize money distributed across teams and players was over ₹50 crore.


  4. Teams

    1. Current teams

      There are 10 teams in the IPL as of 2025. Teams represent different cities/states, and each has a mix of Indian and overseas players.

      • Chennai Super Kings (CSK)
      • Mumbai Indians (MI)
      • Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB)
      • Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR)
      • Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH)
      • Delhi Capitals (DC)
      • Rajasthan Royals (RR)
      • Punjab Kings (PBKS)
      • Lucknow Super Giants (LSG)
      • Gujarat Titans (GT)
    2. Defunct teams

      Several teams were terminated or suspended due to financial, legal, or ownership issues.

      • Deccan Chargers (2008–2012) – Terminated due to financial issues.
      • Kochi Tuskers Kerala (2011) – Terminated after 1 season due to contract breach.
      • Pune Warriors India (2011–2013) – Withdrew due to financial disputes.
      • Rising Pune Supergiant and Gujarat Lions (2016–2017) – Temporary teams during CSK & RR suspension.
    3. Timeline

      IPL started in 2008 and was won by Rajasthan Royals. In 2022, the IPL expanded to 10 teams with the addition of Lucknow Super Giants and Gujarat Titans.


  5. Tournament seasons and results

    1. Performance in the IPL by title

      Mumbai Indians (MI) and Chennai Super Kings (CSK) are the most successful teams, each with 5 IPL titles.

    2. IPL season results

      Rajasthan Royals won the first IPL season in 2008. RCB won their first IPL title in 2025, ending a long wait.


  6. Teams performance

    1. League stage positions

      Sunrisers Hyderabad and Chennai Super Kings both finished top of the table with 18 points, qualifying for the playoffs. Kolkata Knight Riders clinched 3rd place with 16 points, followed by Rajasthan Royals in 4th with 14 points—these four teams advanced to the playoff stage.


  7. Awards

    1. Orange Cap

      Kane Williamson (Sunrisers Hyderabad) won the Orange Cap by scoring 735runs in 17 innings, averaging 52.50 with a strike rate of 142.44

    2. Purple Cap

      Andrew Tye (Kings XI Punjab) won the Purple Cap in 2018 by taking 24 wickets in 14 matches, finishing as the season's highest wicket‑taker, with a bowling average of 18.66 and an economy rate of 8.00.

    3. Most Valuable Player

      Sunil Narine of Kolkata Knight Riders earned the IPL 2018 MVP, scoring 357 runs and taking 17 wickets, accumulating a total of 379.5 points, leading the points chart by a healthy margin

    4. Faimlay Award

      Mumbai Indians won the Fair Play Award in IPL 2018, earning the trophy for exhibiting the best on-field conduct throughout the season.The award is based on umpires’ ratings—teams can score up to 10 points per match assessing factors like spirit of the game, respect for opposition, laws, and officials.

    5. Emerging player award

      Rishabh Pant (Delhi Daredevils) was named the Emerging Player of the Season, finishing with 684 runs in 14 matches at an average of ~52.6 and a strike rate of 173.60. He also won the Stylish Player award, making it a double honor on the final night

    6. Most scores Award

      KL Rahul (Kings XI Punjab) topped the tournament in sheer six-frequency, launching 32 sixes in his 14 innings at a strike rate of around 141, closely followed by Andre Russell who smashed 31 sixes in the competition.


  8. Financials

    1. Title Sponsorship

      The IPL title sponsor changes over the years—DLF (2008–2012), Pepsi (2013–2015), Vivo (2016–2021), TATA (2022–present). TATA Group currently holds the title sponsorship for IPL, with a reported deal worth ₹670 crore for 2022–2023, extended further due to positive association.

    2. Payments to foreign national boards

      The BCCI pays a fixed fee to foreign cricket boards (like ECB, CA, NZC) for allowing their players to participate in the IPL. This fee acts as compensation for player availability and ranges in crores depending on the number and popularity of players from that country.

    3. Brand value

      The IPL brand value in 2024 was estimated at over $10.7 billion, reflecting its massive commercial growth and global appeal. Teams like Mumbai Indians and Chennai Super Kings are among the most valuable franchises, each valued at over $100 million.


  9. Broadcasting

    1. Sony and WSG (2008-2017)

      In 2008, Sony Pictures Networks and World Sports Group (WSG) jointly secured the IPL media rights for 10 years (2008–2017) with a bid of $1.026 billion. Sony was the official broadcaster in India during this period, making IPL a TV sensation, while WSG managed international distribution and sponsorship rights.

    2. Star India(2018-2022)

      In 2017, Star India won the global media rights for IPL (2018–2022) with a record bid of ₹16,347.5 crore (approx. $2.55 billion). This deal gave Star India exclusive rights to broadcast and digitally stream IPL matches worldwide, significantly boosting its reach via Star Sports and Disney+ Hotstar.

    3. 2023-2027

      Disney Star (Star Sports) secured the TV rights for the Indian subcontinent at approximately ₹23,575 crore (≈ ₹57.5 crore per match), while Viacom18 won the digital rights for ₹20,500 crore (≈ ₹50 crore per match).The total media rights for IPL over the 2023–2027 cycle were sold for about ₹48,390 crore, nearly tripling the previous deal, making IPL the second‑most valuable sports property globally after the NFL.

    4. International broadcasters

      IPL global broadcast rights are managed via Viacom18 and sub-licensing deals using partners like DAZN (whose deal reportedly faced issues post‑2023).The region-wise media coverage tier ensures almost all cricket-loving markets get access either via regional broadcasters or global streaming (esp. YuppTV and Willow TV).


  10. Controversy

    1. 2012 and 2013 IPL spot facing and betting match

      In IPL 2013, Delhi Police revealed that Rajasthan Royals player Ajit Chandila claimed to have informed bookies that KKR would beat RCB on May 12 (Ranchi), a match KKR won by four wickets; that game was put under investigation but no subsequent proof linked KKR players to match‑fixing.

    2. Sponsorship

      Sponsorship deals contribute significantly to IPL’s revenue, with Title Sponsorships alone worth hundreds of crores (TATA’s deal: ₹670 crore for 2022–2023).
